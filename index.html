Calculation details

The tool first smooths the elevation trace over a distance window, then computes cumulative ascent with a hysteresis threshold so small elevation wiggles are ignored. For example, +800 m, -100 m, +200 m counts as 1000 m of ascent.

Distance uses the haversine formula. Average grade is cumulative ascent divided by segment distance. Max grade is shown in the page stats only, using the steepest positive grade over a 100 m rolling window.
